About Me

Hi, and welcome! I appreciate you stopping by. My name is Divya and I am a first year Master’s student at Teacher’s College, Columbia University studying Psychological Counseling with a specialization in Mental Health Counseling. As an aspiring mental health counselor, I hope to use my abilities of effective listening, compassion, and desire to understand the spoken and unspoken emotions of others to walk alongside my clients. I am passionate about understanding the unique background of each person that I help as I walk alongside them and it is my hope that I can make an impact as I carry this out as a counselor.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Psychology and a minor in Communications from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and have participated in various counseling roles over the past few years that have had a great impact on my clinical development. I learned how to stay calm in the midst of crisis through my role as a crisis hotline counselor. My ability to stay calm in crisis led me into my role as an applied behavior analysis therapist where I worked with children on the autism spectrum and helped them master diverse coping skills. My experience shadowing a psychotherapist gave me a glimpse to real life counseling scenarios as I grasped the process of clinical supervision and the importance of reviewing case studies. I had a clearer understanding of a counseling role when I was one of five undergraduate students chosen to enroll in a graduate-level counseling pre-practicum course. In this course, I started to develop my professional and counseling skills during my weekly counseling sessions with a volunteer client followed with supervision. You can learn more about my experience in my Curriculum Vitae.

Among all my experiences, I have gained much knowledge in the mental health field that has fueled my dedication to this field. I am eager to continue learning about the field, dive further into my coursework, advance my clinical skills through fieldwork, practicum, and supervision and immerse myself in academic and empirical research.
